Authentic Rwanda

Where Culture Lives

Rwanda's cultural villages offer more than just performances—they provide genuine connections to living traditions. Through hands-on workshops, storytelling sessions, and community interactions, visitors gain deep insights into the customs, crafts, and daily life that have shaped Rwandan identity for generations.

Featured Experience

Iby'Iwacu Cultural Village

Musanze • Volcanoes National Park

Located near Volcanoes National Park, Iby'Iwacu (meaning "Our Home") was established by former poachers who now showcase traditional Rwandan life. Experience the dramatic Intore dance performed by warriors in elaborate headdresses, try your hand at traditional archery, learn the art of banana beer brewing, and participate in a mock royal ceremony. The village demonstrates how communities have transformed from conflict to conservation.

Rwesero Arts Museum

Nyanza • Southern Province

This contemporary art center celebrates Rwanda's artistic evolution. View works by King Mutara III Rudahigwa, explore modern Rwandan art, and watch artists at work. The museum bridges traditional and contemporary expression.

Banda Cultural Village

Nyanza • Southern Province

Immerse yourself in authentic village life near the former royal capital. Learn traditional pottery techniques, weave Agaseke peace baskets, and hear oral histories from community elders. The village offers overnight stays in traditional huts.
