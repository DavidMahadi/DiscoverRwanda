Aquatic Wonders

Where Water Meets Wonder

Rwanda's lakes are more than just bodies of water—they're sanctuaries of serenity, playgrounds for adventure, and windows into the country's rich biodiversity. From the vast expanse of Lake Kivu to the intimate charm of the Twin Lakes, each waterway offers its own unique character and unforgettable experiences.

01

LAKE KIVU

Western Rwanda • One of Africa's Great Lakes

Rwanda's largest and most spectacular lake stretches along the western border with the DRC. With crystal-clear waters, sandy beaches, and a stunning mountain backdrop, Lake Kivu is a paradise for relaxation and water sports. Its peaceful shores host charming resort towns and breathtaking sunsets.

TWIN LAKES

Northern Rwanda • Burera & Ruhondo

Nestled at the foot of the Virunga volcanoes, Lakes Burera and Ruhondo are connected waterways surrounded by terraced hillsides and mountain peaks. These serene lakes offer a peaceful escape with stunning panoramic views, perfect for canoeing, fishing, and birdwatching.

03

LAKE MUHAZI

Eastern Province • 60km Long

This long, winding lake stretches through eastern Rwanda, creating countless coves and peninsulas perfect for exploration. Lake Muhazi is a popular weekend destination for Kigali residents, offering excellent fishing, water sports, and lakeside resorts.

04

LAKE IHEMA

Akagera National Park • Wildlife Paradise

The largest lake in Akagera National Park, Lake Ihema is a wildlife haven where hippos wallow, crocodiles bask, and hundreds of bird species congregate. Surrounded by papyrus swamps and acacia woodlands, exceptional boat safaris bring you face-to-face with elephants, buffalo, and giraffes at the shore.
